01

The problem

Re-platform the checkout without disrupting current shoppers.

A trusted retailer's checkout, used by millions, sat on infrastructure that was holding back new payment methods. Customers wanted PayPal and BNPL; the platform needed to move to microservices.

The challenge was delivery: ship modern payments and migrate the foundations at the same time, on a flow where any downtime is lost revenue and lost trust.

02

The approach

I

Integrate new methods

Integrated PayPal and BNPL into the primary checkout, aligning external partners, risk compliance, and engineering squads to capture new revenue streams.

II

Lead the migration

Led the architectural migration to GCP microservices, executing a phased cutover strategy to modernise infrastructure, whilst limiting operational risk.

III

Protect the flow

Maintained 100% platform uptime and basket stability for the high-volume checkout across peak Black Friday trading.

03

The outcome

PayPal and BNPL live in JohnLewis.com checkout, improving purchase conversion for one of the UK's largest retailers, on modernised, GCP-microservices infrastructure delivered end-to-end by the DevOps team I managed.
