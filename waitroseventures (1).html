Venture 01 — The idea
Groceries delivered, unpacked and put away while you're out.
An industry-first service that asked customers to trust a retailer inside their home. Taken from a scrappy MVP to a national line of business.
Venture 01 01

While You're Away — the problem

An unproven proposition with high operational complexity and no market validation.

Letting a retailer deliver inside your home is a big ask of customer trust, and an even bigger one of operations. The concept existed as an MVP, but the demand, the funnel and the model were unproven.

The job: validate it with real customers, then turn a venture experiment into a repeatable national business.

Venture 01 02

The approach

I

Validate with customers

Ran 0→1 discovery to test demand and de-risk an industry-first proposition before scaling spend.

II

Test operational complexity

Partnered with a third-party security provider to install smart locks on customer homes, integrating secure, one-time driver access into the delivery system. Managed an offshore development team to build and ship the integration.

III

Confirm if the market is ready

Conducted product-market fit validation to measure user reliance on in-home delivery and assess operational scalability.

Venture 01 03

The outcome

Scaled the service to several hundred Waitrose customers. However, post-launch evaluation revealed significant operational friction. Real-world edge cases like pets, deep user trust barriers, and limited market readiness ultimately showed the proposition lacked a viable path to scale.

  • The underlying need was real: customers genuinely wanted groceries handled without having to be home for them.
  • In-home delivery wasn't the way to solve it: the trust and operational cost of a stranger entering the home outweighed the convenience it bought back.

Venture 02 — The idea
A guided wine tasting, delivered to your door.
A direct-to-consumer MVP offering curated wine flights for at-home tasting, scaled from a small trial into a standalone national business.
Venture 02 01

Wine Tasting at Home — the problem

A promising MVP with weak conversion and no proven pricing model.

The trial had real early interest, customers wanted a guided tasting experience without leaving the house, but enquiries weren't converting, and the venture had no evidence on what to charge or how to package it.

The job: fix the funnel and find a price and package that customers would actually pay for, then prove the model could scale beyond a trial.

Venture 02 02

The approach

I

Diagnose the funnel

Mapped the enquiry-to-booking journey to find where genuinely interested customers were dropping off and why.

II

Experiment on package and price

Ran pricing and bundling experiments across flight sizes, occasion framing and gifting to find the highest-converting package.

III

Prove it with real customers

Tracked satisfaction and repeat intent directly through Trustpilot, using genuine reviews as the signal to scale beyond trial.

Venture 02 03

The outcome

Package and pricing experimentation lifted enquiry conversion by 50%, and the experience itself held up, earning 150+ five-star Trustpilot reviews. The venture graduated from a small trial into a standalone national business line.
